<Section icon={Map} title="Himeko Nova Review: Mechanics and Assist Skills">

Himeko Nova introduces a revolutionary playstyle to the 2026 meta, moving away from traditional turn-based constraints by introducing the **Assist Skill** button. Unlike standard Erudition units that rely solely on follow-up attacks or raw Ultimate damage, Himeko Nova functions as a battlefield commander. When she enters the field, she deploys the **Starblazer Territory**, summoning the Starblazer entity. This entity grants the entire team an Assist Skill usage, which essentially acts as an extra attack button that does not consume the ally's natural turn if used correctly.

The Assist Skill deals AoE damage followed by three bounce attacks. If Himeko uses it on her own turn, the multipliers increase significantly, and she doesn't consume the team's shared Assist use. This makes her turn economy incredibly efficient. Furthermore, using an Assist Skill with a **Trailblaze Companion** triggers an immediate extra turn for that ally, ensuring your rotation never slows down.

Always prioritize using Assist Skills on **Trailblaze Companions**. This grants them an immediate extra turn (A4 Trace), allowing you to battery Himeko's energy without losing momentum in high-difficulty content.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Dual States: Verdict vs. Decimation">

A core part of any **himeko nova review** is understanding her two distinct combat states. Depending on which Trailblaze Companion activates the Assist Skill, Himeko will enter either the **Verdict** or **Decimation** state. Each state offers unique buffs and conditions for triggering free Assist Skills, making your team composition the most important factor in her performance.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Verdict State">
    - **Trigger**: MC, Dan Heng, or Sunday.
    - **Buffs**: +100% DMG and +100% Ultimate DMG.
    - **Free Use**: Triggered after allies use 2 Ultimates.
    - **Best For**: Single-target bosses and Elite encounters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Decimation State">
    - **Trigger**: March 7th, Welt, or Base Himeko.
    - **Buffs**: +100% CRIT DMG for all allies and Skills.
    - **Free Use**: Triggered after hitting 9 enemies.
    - **Best For**: Pure Fiction and multi-target waves.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Choosing the right state is situational. Verdict turns Himeko into a tactical nuker, perfect for deleting bosses with her Ultimate. Decimation, on the other hand, provides massive team-wide Crit buffs, making it the superior choice for clearing waves of smaller enemies or when running dual-DPS setups with units like Blade.

| State | Primary Buff | Trigger Requirement | Ideal Content |
|-------|--------------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Verdict** | **200% Total DMG** | 2 Ally Ultimates | Echo of War / MoC |
| **Decimation** | **200% Crit DMG** | 9 Enemy Hits | Pure Fiction / Calyx |

<Callout type="warning" title="State Overlap">
At E0, you can only trigger these free Assist Skills twice per Ultimate. The count resets only when Himeko Nova uses her Ultimate, so timing your bursts is critical.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Best Build: Relics and Stat Priorities">

To achieve peak performance, Himeko Nova requires specific relic sets introduced in the 2026 updates. Her damage is split between her Skill (Assist Skill) and her Ultimate, meaning sets that buff both are highly valued. The **Navigator Isee Sees It** set is her undisputed best-in-slot, as it provides a massive 54% DMG bonus that is easily maintained due to her frequent Assist Skill usage.

| Slot | Recommended Set / Stat | Priority |
|------|------------------------|----------|
| **Relic Set** | **Navigator Isee Sees It** | **High** |
| **Planar Set** | **Fallen Star / Bananamusement** | **Medium** |
| **Body** | **Crit Rate** (Aim for 100% in combat) | **High** |
| **Feet** | **ATK%** (If paired with Sunday/Sparkle) | **Medium** |
| **Orb** | **ATK% or Fire DMG%** | **Medium** |
| **Rope** | **ATK%** (Energy Rope for S0 comfort) | **Low** |

Her stat goals are quite demanding. Because she self-buffs Crit Damage significantly through her traces and the Decimation state, you should almost always run a **Crit Rate chest**. Aiming for 100% Crit Rate in-combat is the gold standard for 2026 endgame builds.

<Callout type="success" title="Stat Benchmarks">

- **Crit Rate**: 80% (Base) / 100% (Buffed)
- **Crit DMG**: 130% - 150%+ (Base)
- **Speed**: Base (96) is fine if using Action Advancers.

<Section icon={Target} title="Light Cone Rankings">

Himeko Nova's signature Light Cone is one of the most overloaded options in the game. It provides a massive **32% DEF Ignore** and helps solve her energy issues by granting 6 Energy per Assist Skill. While F2P options exist, the gap between her signature and generic Erudition cones is noticeable in 2026's high-HP meta.

| Light Cone | Tier | Key Benefits |
|------------|------|--------------|
| **Signature S1** | **S+** | **32% DEF Ignore**, 60% DMG, Energy Regen |
| **The Herta (Amphoreus)** | **A** | High Base Stats, Solid F2P alternative |
| **Night on the Milky Way** | **B+** | Great for Pure Fiction, weaker in ST |
| **Today is Another Peaceful Day** | **B** | Consistent DMG, but lacks utility |

If you are a low-spender, prioritizing her Signature S1 is often more cost-effective than chasing E1, as it provides both a damage ceiling increase and much-needed rotational comfort.

<Callout type="info" title="Light Cone Tip">
If you lack her signature, the Herta Shop cone from the Amphoreus update is your best bet. Avoid using low-rarity cones as Himeko's base ATK scaling is vital for her Assist Skill multipliers.
</Callout>
